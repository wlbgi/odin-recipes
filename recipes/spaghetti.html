Spaghetti Bolognese Recipe

Description

This is a simple spaghetti with Bolognese sauce recipe for a chunky sauce with beef, pork, vegetables, and lots of flavor. This recipe makes 9 servings and takes a total of 1 hour and 30 minutes. Sauce can be frozen for later, making this an excellent dish to prepare in advance for later in the week. This recipe was taken from allrecipes.com.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 4 slices bacon, cut into 1/2 inch pieces
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 (28 ounce) can Italian plum tomatos
  • 1/2 pound fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 6 ounces tomato sauce
  • 2 carrots, shredded
  • 1 stalk celery, chopped
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound pasta

Steps

  1. Warm oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Saut√© onion, bacon, and garlic in hot oil until bacon is browned and crisp, about 10 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer bacon mixture to a paper towel-lined plate to drain; set aside.
  2. Place the same large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir ground beef and pork in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain and discard grease.
  3. Stir bacon mixture, tomatoes, mushrooms, tomato sauce, carrots, celery, wine, stock, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper into meat mixture. Bring to a boil. Cover, reduce heat, and simmer sauce for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  4. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook in boiling water until al dente, 8 to 10 minutes; drain.
  5. Serve sauce over hot pasta.
